About This Book

Data-Driven Management: Statistics for Managers was written for students who will use statistics to inform decisions, not to become statisticians. The emphasis throughout is on understanding what a result means and what to do with it.

How the book is organised

The book moves in eight parts:

  1. Foundations - what statistics is, how data is structured, and how samples relate to populations.
  2. Describing Data - summarising and visualising distributions.
  3. Probability - the logic that connects samples to populations.
  4. Relationships - correlation and regression.
  5. Estimation - confidence intervals and the central limit theorem.
  6. Hypothesis Testing Logic - the framework shared by all inferential tests.
  7. Core Tests - the most commonly used procedures.
  8. Interpretation and Decision Use - choosing the right test and reading output correctly.

**Data-Driven Management: Statistics for Managers** was written for students who will use statistics to inform decisions, not to become statisticians. The emphasis throughout is on understanding what a result means and what to do with it.

6. **Hypothesis Testing Logic** - the framework shared by all inferential tests.
7. **Core Tests** - the most commonly used procedures.
