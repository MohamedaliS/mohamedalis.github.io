Instructor Pack: Unit 08 — Mean, Median, Mode, Variance, and Standard Deviation

Session overview

Duration: 75 minutes Dataset: ch08_main.csv Slides: Block 02, lecture 08 Worked example: ex08

Pacing guide

Pacing guide for Unit 08
Time Activity Notes
0-5 min Welcome and recap of Unit 7 Ask one student to summarise the previous unit in one sentence.
5-20 min Core lecture: follow slide deck Pause at each plot slide; ask students to interpret before explaining.
20-35 min Worked example: students work through ex08 Steps 1-2 together; Steps 3-5 in pairs.
35-55 min Discussion questions (see below) DQ1 whole class; DQ2 small groups; DQ3 optional.
55-70 min Dataset exploration in R Open ch08_main.csv; explore with summary() and key functions.
70-75 min Summary and preview of next unit Name the one concept most likely to appear in the assessment.

Learning objectives

Calculate and interpret mean, median, and mode; explain when each is appropriate; calculate variance and standard deviation; interpret SD in context; use measures together to describe a distribution.

Discussion questions

DQ1 (whole class, 5 min): A supermarket reports that the mean weekly spend of its loyalty card holders is GBP 87. A second report says the median is GBP 54. What does this difference tell you about the distribution of spending? Which figure is more useful for planning stock levels?

DQ2 (small groups, 7 min): Two investment portfolios have the same mean annual return of 8%. Portfolio A has a standard deviation of 2%; Portfolio B has a standard deviation of 14%. How should a risk-averse investor interpret this information?

DQ3 (challenge, optional): A colleague calculates the standard deviation using n rather than n-1 in the denominator and gets a slightly smaller result. Does it matter? When would the difference be meaningful?

Common misconceptions

Misconception 1: Students often cannot interpret standard deviation directly. Give them a concrete example: call duration mean 4.2 mins, SD 1.8 mins. Ask what a SD of 1.8 means in practical terms for staffing.

Misconception 2: Students confuse variance (squared units, hard to interpret) with standard deviation (original units). Always show both and explain why SD is the reported measure in practice.

Dataset notes

ch08_main.csv: 200 call centre interactions. Use call_duration_mins for mean, median, SD; team for group comparisons; customer_satisfaction for ordinal summary discussion.

Assessment

Moodle quiz: quiz08.xml aligned to unit 08 objectives. Assign after the session as a formative check before the next unit.
